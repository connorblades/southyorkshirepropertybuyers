### How to respond to a journalist request

1. **Speed is everything.** First three good responses usually win. Aim to respond within 4 hours.
2. **Stick to the brief.** Answer their actual question; no PR fluff.
3. **Lead with the quote.** Two to three sentences they can paste directly into the article.
4. **Add credentials in one line.** "Connor Blades is founder of South Yorkshire Property Buyers, a Sheffield cash buyer."
5. **Include the link they should use.** Specify exact anchor text + URL.
6. **No attachments.** They'll ask if they want more.

### Example response template (for a "cash buyer warnings" type query)

> Hi *[journalist]*,
>
> On your query about red flags when selling to a cash buyer:
>
> "The single biggest warning sign is any company that asks for an upfront fee. A legitimate UK cash buyer charges nothing — the offer you're given is the amount you receive at completion. The second is last-minute price reductions, where an attractive headline offer gets quietly cut just before exchange. A reputable buyer only revises an offer if a survey or legal search uncovers something genuinely material."
>
> Connor Blades, Founder, South Yorkshire Property Buyers — a Sheffield cash buyer working across South Yorkshire. Happy to expand on any of this. If a link is useful, our guide is at [southyorkshirepropertybuyers.com/blog/cash-buyer-scams-uk-how-to-spot-a-legitimate-buyer/](https://southyorkshirepropertybuyers.com/blog/cash-buyer-scams-uk-how-to-spot-a-legitimate-buyer/).
>
> Connor

- **Citizens Advice Sheffield** — resource pages on selling to avoid repossession

These will only link if you build the relationship first. Long-game targets, not week-one priorities.

## Master tracking spreadsheet (recommended)

Once you start outreach, move this list into a spreadsheet (Google Sheets) with columns:
- Date contacted
- Channel (email / LinkedIn / form)
- Response (yes / no / no reply)
- Follow-up date
- Outcome (link earned / dead / pending)

After 60 days, your hit rate will tell you which categories work — invest more effort there.
