# TITLE

South Yorkshire Homeowners Warned as UK Repossessions Rise 39% and Landlord Exits Hit Record Levels

---

# CONTENT

Local cash buying firm calls for greater awareness of seller options as national data reveals a sharp rise in mortgage possession claims across England.

Mortgage repossessions across the UK rose by 39% in 2025, with 5,160 mortgaged properties repossessed over the year according to government Ministry of Justice statistics. Homeowner repossessions rose by 51% year on year in the third quarter of 2025 alone, the sharpest quarterly rise recorded since before the pandemic.

At the same time, an estimated 93,000 buy-to-let landlords exited the private rental sector in 2025, with a further 110,000 predicted to follow in 2026 according to broker survey data. The Renters' Rights Act, which came into force in late 2025 and abolished Section 21 no-fault evictions, has been cited as a tipping point for many smaller landlords already under pressure from rising mortgage costs.

Connor Blades, founder of South Yorkshire Property Buyers (https://southyorkshirepropertybuyers.com), says the local picture reflects the national trend.

"We are seeing more enquiries from homeowners in South Yorkshire who have reached the point where they cannot see a way forward through the traditional property market. Repossession proceedings, landlords who need to sell with tenants in place, people going through divorce or dealing with an inherited property they cannot afford to maintain. The common thread is that they need to move quickly and they do not know where to turn.

"The problem is that most people in that position have never heard of a cash buyer, or they have heard of them but assume they are a last resort or a scam. What we are trying to do is make that option more visible and more transparent so that people can make an informed decision before the situation gets worse.

"For many sellers, a fast cash sale at 80 to 85 percent of market value is a better outcome than a repossession on their record, months of ongoing mortgage payments on a property they cannot sell, or a prolonged legal dispute with a tenant. The numbers often work in their favour. But they need to know that option exists."
