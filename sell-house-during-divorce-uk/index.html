Can you sell your house during a divorce in the UK?

The short answer is yes, but both parties must agree. Understanding the legal picture and choosing the right route makes an enormous difference to how smoothly it goes.

The short answer: yes, but it requires agreement

If you jointly own a property with your spouse or civil partner, both parties must agree to the sale. One party cannot force a sale without a court order. However, if both parties want to sell and move on, the process can be straightforward provided you have the right support in place.

What happens to the property in a divorce?

The matrimonial home is usually the largest asset in a divorce. There are broadly three outcomes: one partner buys out the other, the property is sold and the proceeds split, or the sale is deferred until children reach a certain age. For most people who need a clean financial break, selling is the simplest route.

The problem with selling on the open market during a divorce

Selling through an estate agent while going through a divorce adds significant stress to an already difficult situation. Viewings require coordination, disagreements about pricing can cause delays, and buyer chains can collapse months in. Each delay prolongs the financial and emotional entanglement between both parties. If you are already struggling to sell on top of this, the pressure compounds quickly.
