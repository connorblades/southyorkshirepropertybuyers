Struggling to sell your house

Feeling stuck is one of the most common experiences in property. Whether the obstacles are financial, practical, emotional, or a combination of all three, there are usually more options available than it feels like right now. You are not out of choices.

Get a Free Cash Offer

This page is written for people who are finding it genuinely hard to sell, not just impatient, but actually stuck. Maybe the market has not responded the way you hoped. Maybe your circumstances have changed and what felt manageable six months ago no longer does. Maybe you have been trying to sell for a long time and the experience has left you exhausted and unsure what to do next. Whatever has brought you here, this page is intended to help you think clearly about what your options actually are and which of them might genuinely move things forward.

There is no single reason people struggle to sell

The word "struggling" covers an enormous range of situations. Some people are struggling because the property itself has problems, including condition issues, structural concerns, an unusual construction type, or legal complications on the title. Some are struggling because of their personal circumstances, such as financial pressure, a relationship breakdown, or a bereavement, or a health situation that has made managing the process overwhelming. Some are struggling because the market in their area or price bracket has simply been slow, and the right buyer has not appeared yet.

Often it is a combination. A property that needs work, owned by someone going through a difficult personal period, listed at slightly the wrong price with an agent who is not actively pushing it. Each individual factor might be manageable in isolation, but together they create a situation that feels impossible to shift.

The emotional reality of a stuck sale

Property is not just a financial asset. For most people, it is bound up with major life events, where they raised children, where they went through hard times, where their life took a particular shape. Selling can feel like loss even when it is the right decision. And when the sale keeps failing or the market keeps not responding, the emotional cost compounds. People describe feeling trapped, embarrassed, and increasingly convinced that the problem is unsolvable.

It is worth naming this directly because it affects decision-making. People stay in situations longer than makes sense financially because the idea of accepting less than they hoped for feels like a failure. But getting unstuck is not failure. Moving forward, even on terms that are not ideal, is almost always better than remaining stuck.

Financial struggles and selling

If financial pressure is part of the picture, the urgency of selling is often higher than the market timeline allows for. A traditional estate agent sale takes months. If you are managing mortgage arrears, covering costs on an empty property, dealing with rent you are not receiving from a difficult tenant, or simply watching savings drain away while a sale drags on, speed becomes essential rather than merely desirable.

In these situations, the question is not "can I get full market value?" but "what is the best outcome I can achieve given how things actually are?" A cash offer at 82 % of market value that completes in two weeks is often a far better financial outcome than waiting six more months for a full-price offer that may or may not materialise.

Practical obstacles and how to address them

If the struggle is primarily practical, such as the property needing work you cannot fund, title issues, or a tenanted property, these are solvable problems with the right buyer. A cash buyer does not need the property to be mortgageable, does not require repairs to be completed, and can work around tenancy situations. If the property has condition issues that have repeatedly caused sales to fall through, a cash sale removes that obstacle entirely. If there are title complications, an experienced cash buyer will have dealt with similar issues before and will not be scared off by them in the way a mortgage-dependent buyer might be.

Similarly, if the property has been on the market for a long time and has become stale, a fresh approach through a different route can sometimes break the deadlock. See our page on what to do when your house won't sell for a fuller breakdown of why properties stall and what tends to move them.
