Selling a house in poor condition: what are your options?

A property in poor condition is not unsellable. Your route to sale matters. Here is an honest comparison of every option, from repairs through auction to a direct cash sale.

Get a Free Cash Offer

Why condition affects your sale more than you think

Most buyers purchasing through an estate agent are using a mortgage. Mortgage lenders require a surveyor to inspect the property before they will offer funds. If the surveyor identifies significant issues, the lender may refuse to lend, reduce the amount they will offer, or impose conditions. This is why a property that looks saleable at full price on Rightmove can still fail to complete.

What kinds of issues cause the most problems?

  • Structural movement or subsidence
  • Damp, including penetrating damp and rising damp
  • Japanese knotweed in or near the garden
  • Roof in significant disrepair
  • Non-standard construction such as prefab or concrete panels
  • Previous underpinning
  • Asbestos in roof sheeting or floor tiles
  • Flood history or high flood risk zone

Option 1: Repair before selling

If you have the time and money, addressing the issues before listing can increase both your sale price and the size of your buyer pool. However, major structural repairs can cost tens of thousands of pounds and take months. There is also no guarantee the work will satisfy a surveyor. For most people who contact us, this option has already been ruled out.

Option 2: Sell at auction

Property auctions attract investors and cash buyers who are more comfortable with risk. Properties sell on the day and completion typically follows within 28 days. The downside is significant auction fees, the possibility your reserve price is not met, and limited ability to control how the property is presented.

Option 3: Sell to a cash buyer directly

Selling directly to a cash buyer like us removes the surveyor from the equation entirely. We carry out our own assessment, make an offer reflecting the condition, and proceed without lender interference. No auction fees, no estate agent commission, and no risk of a mortgage-related collapse. We buy properties across South Yorkshire in any condition. See however much work is needed.

If your property has been on the market and won't sell because of condition-related issues, this is typically the cleanest and fastest resolution.
