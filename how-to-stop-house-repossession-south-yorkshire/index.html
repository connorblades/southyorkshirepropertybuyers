How to stop a house repossession in South Yorkshire

Repossession proceedings have stages, and at almost every one of them you still have options. Acting early is the single biggest factor in a good outcome.

What is repossession and when does it happen?

Repossession occurs when a mortgage lender takes back ownership of your property because you have fallen behind on your payments. It is a last resort for lenders, but once proceedings begin they can move quickly. In South Yorkshire, as across the rest of England and Wales, the lender must apply to the court for a possession order before they can take your home.

What are the stages of repossession?

  • Missed payments and default notices from your lender
  • Formal letter before action
  • Court claim for possession
  • Possession hearing at your local county court
  • Possession order granted (often with a 28-day suspension)
  • Warrant of possession and eviction if arrears are not cleared

At each stage there are things you can do. The earlier you act, the more options are available to you.

Your options at each stage

Before court proceedings begin, contact your lender directly. Many have hardship arrangements, payment holidays, or can switch you to interest-only temporarily. You can also contact a free debt advice service such as StepChange or National Debtline.

If a court date has already been set, you can request a suspension or adjournment if you have a realistic plan to clear the arrears. This could be through remortgaging, selling the property, or a payment plan.

If your property has equity and you need to sell quickly, a cash buyer is often the fastest way to clear the debt before the court acts. A traditional estate agent sale takes an average of 4 to 6 months in the UK. A cash sale can complete in as little as 7 to 14 days.
