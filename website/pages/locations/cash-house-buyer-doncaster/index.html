What "cash house buyer" actually means in Doncaster

A cash house buyer is the principal purchaser, not a broker. They use their own funds, held in a UK solicitor's client account or as cleared bank balances evidenced by a regulated UK bank, to purchase your property without any mortgage. That is the only working definition. Anyone who calls themselves a cash buyer but is actually arranging finance, leading you to a panel of investors, or selling your details on as a lead is not a cash buyer in any honest sense. The Doncaster market is large: 568 km² and 308,100 residents, the largest metropolitan borough in England by land area, and the cash-buyer cohort here is mixed. Some are genuine regional operators like us. Some are national brands. Some are unregulated middlemen. This page is about how you tell the difference. Six checks every Doncaster seller should make before accepting a cash offer

Run these on us. Run them on every other cash buyer you approach. None of them costs anything. Together they take roughly thirty minutes. They are the difference between a clean sale and the kind of regret that ends up in a Doncaster Free Press warning piece.

1. Companies House registration. Search the company name and number at find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk. Confirm the company is active (not "in administration" or "dissolution proposed"), that the registered office matches what the buyer states, that the persons of significant control are named, and that accounts and confirmation statements are current.

2. Proof of funds via a solicitor's letter on letterhead. Ask for a letter from the buyer's solicitor, on the firm's letterhead, dated within fourteen days of your offer, confirming cleared funds in the solicitor's client account sufficient to complete the purchase. The letter should give the solicitor's SRA number. A bank-statement screenshot is not enough, screenshots are trivially forged. A solicitor's letter carries professional indemnity insurance behind it.

3. Solicitor verification. Take the solicitor's name and SRA number from that letter and cross-check them at the Law Society's Find a Solicitor directory. Confirm the firm is currently regulated, in good standing, and the named individual is authorised to practice. This is a thirty-second check.

4. Independent trading-history check. Search the company name plus "review", "scam", and "complaint" on Google. Look at Trustpilot and Google reviews directly (not at the screenshots on the buyer's website). Be wary of brand-new Trustpilot profiles, sudden bursts of five-star reviews with similar wording (a documented fake-review pattern), or "team" photos that reverse-image-search to stock libraries.

5. Your right to use your own solicitor, in writing. This is the single most load-bearing trust signal in any cash sale. The SRA Code of Conduct (section 6.2) prohibits one firm acting for both sides where their interests conflict, and the parties' interests always conflict in a property sale. The seller's solicitor protects the seller; the buyer's solicitor protects the buyer. A cash buyer who insists you use their solicitor, or refuses to confirm your right of choice in writing, has failed this check. Walk away.

6. Citizens Advice Doncaster or any independent second opinion. Free, impartial, government-backed. Citizens Advice Doncaster operates an appointment-based housing-advice service at Priory Place, Doncaster DN1 1TT. Call 0808 278 7891. They will not endorse any specific buyer, but they will explain what a legitimate transaction looks like and flag anything in your contract that does not. The FCA sale-and-rent-back warning every Doncaster seller needs to know

If any cash buyer offers to purchase your Doncaster house and let you stay on as a tenant, stop. This arrangement, known as "sale-and-rent-back" or SRB, has been a regulated activity under the Financial Conduct Authority since 30 June 2010. Any firm offering it must be FCA authorised and listed on the FCA register at register.fca.org.uk.

The FCA imposed effective moratoria on new SRB business through 2010 to 2012 after thousands of consumers lost their homes following firm failures, evictions, and rents being raised to unaffordable levels. As at 2026, the regulated SRB market is effectively dormant. We are not aware of any UK firm currently offering new SRB agreements to consumers under FCA authorisation. That means if anyone, particularly someone targeting Doncaster repossession-route sellers, offers to "buy your house and let you stay", they are very likely operating illegally under section 19 of the Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (the general prohibition on carrying out regulated activities without authorisation). Section 23 makes this a criminal offence with up to two years' imprisonment.

South Yorkshire Property Buyers does not offer sale-and-rent-back. We are not FCA authorised for that activity, and we will not be.
