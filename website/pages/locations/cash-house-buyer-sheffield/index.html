Six checks every Sheffield seller should make before accepting a cash offer

The UK quick-sale industry is not regulated as a sector. There is no statutory licence to set up a cash-buying company and start advertising in Sheffield, which is precisely why the burden of verification sits with the seller. Sheffield City Council Trading Standards has explicitly warned that rogue traders moved online during and after the pandemic behind "professional-looking websites which appear genuine, but many are not". The six checks below take about twenty minutes total and filter out the bad actors. Run them on us. Run them on every Sheffield cash buyer you approach.

1. Companies House

Search the buyer's exact legal name (not the trading name) at find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk. Look for incorporation date (a brand-new shell is a red flag), filing history (have they filed accounts on time?), registered office, named directors, and any People with Significant Control. Worked example: searching "Bullseye Properties Ltd" returns company 14869608, incorporated 15 May 2023, previously named Lord CNB Properties Ltd until 18 April 2024. Direct link: find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk/company/14869608.

2. Dated solicitor's letter as proof of funds

Ask for a solicitor's letter on the solicitor's letterhead, dated within the last 14 days, confirming the buyer has cleared funds available for the purchase. Do not accept a screenshot of a bank balance — screenshots are trivially faked. A bank statement on its own is weaker than a solicitor's letter because the balance can move the next day. Verify the solicitor by calling the firm's switchboard number from the Law Society Find a Solicitor register, not a number the buyer gives you. If a Sheffield cash buyer refuses to provide a dated solicitor's letter on letterhead, walk away.

3. Independent reviews — and a reverse image check

Search Google Reviews and Trustpilot for the trading name and the legal name. Read the one-star and two-star reviews carefully; they tell you more than the five-stars. Search the company name plus "scam" and the company name plus "complaint" on Google. If the "team" page on the buyer's website includes photos, reverse-image-search them on Google Images to confirm they are real people associated with the business and not stock images or someone else's LinkedIn headshot.

4. The Property Ombudsman (TPO) membership

The Property Ombudsman is the approved consumer redress scheme for residential property buying companies. Membership means the firm has signed up to the TPO Code of Practice and operates a written complaints procedure. Verify any claimed membership on the public register at tpos.co.uk. If a company displays a TPO logo but is not on the register, that is a serious red flag — and itself reportable to TPO.

5. Your absolute right to your own solicitor

This is the most important single sentence on this page: you have the absolute right to instruct your own solicitor for the sale, regardless of who is buying. A legitimate cash buyer will support that freely. A buyer who insists you must use only their nominated solicitor as a non-negotiable condition is removing a layer of independent oversight, and that is a red flag. We cover legal fees when sellers use our panel solicitors, but the seller's right to choose their own is preserved and is never discouraged. The Sheffield solicitors named in the next section are starting points; any qualified conveyancer on the Law Society Find a Solicitor register works.

6. One independent voice before you sign

Before signing anything binding, speak to one of the three independent voices a Sheffield seller can consult, all with no commercial interest in the outcome:

  • Citizens Advice Sheffield — freephone Adviceline 0808 278 7820 (Mon to Fri, 10am to 4pm), with face-to-face drop-in advice every Wednesday morning 10am to 12pm at the Community Access Point in Howden House, 1 Union Street, Sheffield S1 2SH.

  • An unconnected Sheffield property solicitor — the three firms named in our solicitors section below, or any CQS-accredited firm on the Law Society register. A thirty-minute consultation costs far less than the difference between a bad deal and a good one.

A buyer who passes all six cleanly is a structured transaction with consumer protections attached. A buyer who fails any of them is not, and the right answer is to walk away — including from us, if those are the answers we give you.

The FCA sale-and-rent-back warning every Sheffield seller needs to read

Sale and rent back is regulated. Anyone offering it without FCA authorisation is committing a criminal offence.

Sale and rent back — where you sell your house quickly to a buyer and stay on as a tenant — has been regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority since 30 June 2010. Any firm offering a sale and rent back deal in Sheffield must be FCA authorised. Selling unauthorised is a criminal offence under the Financial Services and Markets Act 2000. If anyone offers to "buy your Sheffield house and let you stay as a tenant" without showing you their FCA authorisation, that is not a quirky marketing pitch — it is unlawful. Walk away. Report them via Report Fraud (the replacement for Action Fraud, launched 4 December 2025) and to the FCA. We do not offer sale and rent back.

The rule exists because sale-and-rent-back was used in the late 2000s to talk distressed sellers out of homes at deep discounts on rolling short tenancies that gave the new owner an easy eviction route months later. The FCA brought the sector under regulation and the count of legitimate firms collapsed. If a Sheffield buyer raises it without showing FCA authorisation, end the conversation.

Frequently asked questions from Sheffield sellers

What is a legitimate cash house buyer in Sheffield?

A buyer using their own funds, listed at Companies House with a verifiable filing history, providing a dated solicitor's letter on letterhead as proof of funds, a member of The Property Ombudsman redress scheme, charging no upfront fees, and supporting your right to your own solicitor.

How do I verify a Sheffield cash buyer in twenty minutes?

Run the six checks: Companies House, dated solicitor's letter as proof of funds, Google and Trustpilot reviews, TPO membership on the public register, your-own-solicitor freedom, and one independent voice — Citizens Advice Sheffield, MoneySavingExpert, or an unconnected Sheffield solicitor.

Is sale and rent back legal in Sheffield?

Only when the firm is authorised by the Financial Conduct Authority. Sale and rent back has been FCA regulated since 30 June 2010. Selling without FCA authorisation is a criminal offence under the Financial Services and Markets Act 2000. We do not offer sale and rent back.

How much do cash buyers pay for a Sheffield house?

Genuine buyers typically pay 75 to 85 percent of current open market value in 2026. On the Sheffield average of around £222,000 (February 2026 UK HPI), expect £177,000 to £188,000. Lower offers reflect material condition, PRC construction, cladding friction, or other saleability issues.

How quickly can a Sheffield cash sale complete?

As fast as 7 days from offer acceptance. A typical Sheffield completion through us is 14 to 21 days. Slower completions on probate or divorce sales are normal where third-party documents are awaited.

Do I have to use the buyer's solicitor?

No. You have the absolute right to your own solicitor. A buyer who insists otherwise as a condition of the offer has crossed a red line.

Do you buy Sheffield flats with EWS1 or cladding issues?

Yes, in most cases. Cladding-affected leasehold flats in S1, S2 Park Hill and S3 are difficult for mortgage buyers, which is precisely why a cash purchase is the realistic exit route until remediation completes.

Do you buy PRC Defective houses in S5 and S35?

Yes. Sheffield has at least 238 houses and 131 self-contained flats classified Defective under Part XVI of the Housing Act 1985 — Cornish, Reema, Unity, Wates — concentrated in S5 (Parson Cross, Shiregreen) and S35. Without a PRC repair certificate the mainstream lender pool is gone and a cash sale is the realistic route.

What does a legitimate buyer never ask for?

No upfront fee from the seller. No deposit from the seller. No binding signature before a written offer. No condition that the seller must use only the buyer's solicitor. Cross any of those lines and the deal is not a deal.
