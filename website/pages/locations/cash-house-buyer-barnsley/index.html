Six checks every Barnsley seller should make before accepting a cash offer

Run these checks on us. Run them on every cash buyer you approach. They are free, they take less than an afternoon, and they will save you from the small minority of operators who hope you will not bother.

1. Companies House — the permanent public record. Search the buyer's company name on find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk. Confirm the company exists, is active (not dissolved or in liquidation), has filed accounts, has identifiable directors, and was not incorporated in the last few months. We are Bullseye Properties Ltd, company number 14869608, incorporated in 2023 and active in England and Wales. The company was previously named Lord CNB Properties Ltd until 18 April 2024 — that name-change is on the public record and we disclose it openly.

2. Proof of funds on a solicitor's letterhead. Not a bank statement screenshot. Not a screenshot from a trading account. A letter from the buyer's solicitor on their letterhead, dated within 14 days of the offer, stating that cleared funds sufficient for the purchase sit in the firm's client account. The letter should include the solicitor's SRA number for cross-verification. Solicitors are required to carry out source-of-funds and source-of-wealth checks under the Money Laundering Regulations 2017 (as amended in 2022 and 2023), so a legitimate cash buyer's solicitor already has this evidence on file. Any buyer who cannot or will not produce this fails verification.

3. SRA-regulated solicitor verification. Ask the buyer to name their solicitor in writing. Cross-check the firm and the named solicitor at the Law Society's Find a Solicitor directory. The firm should be in good standing with the Solicitors Regulation Authority. If the buyer's "solicitor" is not on the SRA register, the transaction is not safe.

4. Trading-history independence check. Read independent reviews — Trustpilot, Google, Companies House filings, the Barnsley Chronicle local-press archive. Look for unedited recent reviews. Be wary of brand-new Trustpilot profiles, profiles with dozens of five-star reviews using similar wording (a documented fake-review pattern), and any company whose entire online presence dates from the last twelve months.

5. Your own solicitor — confirmed in writing. A legitimate buyer will confirm in writing that you may use any solicitor of your choosing. The SRA Code of Conduct (Standards and Regulations, paragraph 6.2) forbids the same solicitor representing both buyer and seller in a property transaction because of the inherent conflict of interest. If you use our panel we cover the legal fees. If you prefer to use your own solicitor you absolutely can, and we recommend it for any transaction over £100,000. Any buyer who pressures you to use their nominated solicitor — particularly one with whom they share an office or fee-share arrangement — fails this check.

6. Three independent voices. Free, no obligation. Citizens Advice Barnsley, Doncaster and Rotherham covers the BDR catchment at citizensadvicebdr.org.uk with the national Adviceline on 0808 278 7891. They will not endorse any specific buyer but will explain what a legitimate transaction should look like. Your own conveyancing solicitor for a paid initial consultation (typically £75 to £150 — worth every penny). And for probate sellers, a STEP-qualified solicitor via the Society of Trust and Estate Practitioners directory.

The FCA sale-and-rent-back warning

One specific pattern Barnsley sellers should treat as a hard red flag is an offer that combines a cash sale with the right to "stay on as a tenant" after completion. This arrangement — known as sale-and-rent-back, or SRB — has been a regulated activity in the UK since 1 July 2010 under the Financial Services and Markets Act 2000. Only firms authorised by the Financial Conduct Authority can lawfully offer it. Any cash buyer offering this without FCA authorisation is committing a criminal offence.

Regulation followed a 2005–2010 FSA investigation that found systematic harm — sellers pressured into below-market prices, tenancy agreements that were not what sellers thought they had signed, and rent increases or evictions shortly after completion that left families homeless having already sold their only asset. SRB became a regulated activity in response. A small number of FCA-authorised firms now provide it under the FCA's supervisory regime.

The practical check is simple. Before accepting any offer involving staying on as a tenant, search the buyer firm on the FCA Financial Services Register. If the firm does not appear, the offer is illegal. Report it to the FCA on 0800 111 6768 and to Action Fraud on 0300 123 2040. We do not offer sale-and-rent-back. If staying as a tenant is what you actually need, your safest route is an FCA-authorised SRB provider — not an unauthorised cash buyer with a side-offer.

Barnsley-specific FAQs

What is a legitimate cash house buyer in Barnsley?

A buyer using their own funds, registered at Companies House with verifiable trading history, using SRA-regulated solicitors, charging no upfront fees, providing solicitor-letterhead proof of funds dated within 14 days, and confirming in writing that you may use any solicitor of your choosing.

How do I verify a Barnsley cash buyer before signing?

Six free checks. Companies House. Proof of funds on solicitor letterhead. SRA verification of the buyer's solicitor. Trading-history independence check (Trustpilot, Google, Companies House filings, local press). Written confirmation you can use your own solicitor. Citizens Advice Barnsley on 0808 278 7891 for an independent second opinion.

Is sale-and-rent-back legal in Barnsley?

Only if offered by a firm authorised by the Financial Conduct Authority. Sale-and-rent-back has been regulated since 1 July 2010. Any unauthorised Barnsley operator offering "stay on as a tenant" is committing a criminal offence. Check register.fca.org.uk before agreeing to anything resembling SRB.

How much do cash buyers offer for Barnsley property?

Typically 75 to 85 percent of current market value in 2026. Lower offers go to properties with material condition issues, S72 ex-mining ground-stability concerns, or saleability problems. On a £140,000 Barnsley semi, expect £105,000 to £119,000. For the full price calculation, see how much do cash buyers offer below market value.

Can I use my own solicitor when selling to a Barnsley cash buyer?

Yes — and you should. The right is statutory. The SRA Code of Conduct forbids one solicitor representing both sides. Local options include Bury & Walkers LLP (Barnsley HQ), Wake Smith LLP, Banner Jones, Switalskis, Atteys Solicitors and Irwin Mitchell.

Do you buy ex-mining property in S72 Goldthorpe, Thurnscoe and Grimethorpe?

Yes. S72 properties often present mortgage-lender challenges including ground-stability concerns flagged on the Coal Authority CON29M. Cash buyers are not constrained by lender criteria.

Yes. S36 Penistone overlaps administratively with Sheffield postcodes but is part of the wider Barnsley catchment. We buy stone cottages, converted barns, modern detached and rural village properties across Penistone, Thurlstone, Hoylandswaine, Cawthorne and Silkstone.

Can you buy a tenanted Barnsley property?

Yes. We buy tenanted properties across S70 to S75 including arrears cases and situations where Ground 1A would otherwise apply. You do not need vacant possession. For the legal framework, see selling tenanted property in 2026.

Are cash buyers regulated in the UK?

Not directly by the FCA in the way mortgage lenders are. The conveyancing solicitors on both sides are regulated by the SRA. The National Association of Property Buyers (NAPB) operates a voluntary code. The strongest verifiable trust anchor available is the Companies House public record.
