The problem with selling a portfolio property by property

Most landlords who want to exit their portfolio assume they have to sell each property individually. That means multiple solicitors, multiple buyers, multiple chains — and months of uncertainty. One sale falling through can hold up the others. Meanwhile you are still managing the portfolio, dealing with tenants, and paying mortgage and maintenance costs on properties you have already mentally moved on from.

There is a better way. We buy portfolios as a single transaction — all properties, all tenancies, all at once. You agree a price for the portfolio as a whole, we handle the legal side, and you walk away with a clean exit.

What we buy

We are experienced portfolio buyers across South Yorkshire and the surrounding areas. We work with landlords who are looking to:

  • Exit the buy-to-let market entirely and realise their equity
  • Sell a portfolio quickly due to financial pressure or mortgage arrears
  • Dissolve a jointly owned portfolio following a divorce or partnership split
  • Transfer a portfolio quickly as part of estate planning or inheritance
  • Offload a portfolio that has become unmanageable or unprofitable
  • Sell before a significant tax event or regulatory change

We buy portfolios of all types: residential terraces, semis, flats, HMOs, mixed-use residential, ex-council stock. Tenanted, vacant, or a mixture of both.

Why sell your portfolio as a job lot?

A job lot sale gives you something individual sales cannot: certainty. When we agree a price for your portfolio, that is the price we pay. There is no chain, no buyer dropping out, no survey renegotiation — because we are the buyer and we have the funds.

You also avoid the compounding costs of a prolonged sales process. Every month a property sits unsold, you are paying mortgage interest, insurance, management fees, and maintenance — and potentially watching the market move against you. A clean, fast exit often makes more financial sense than holding out for individual sale prices that may never materialise.

How the portfolio sale process works

Step 1: Tell us about your portfolio. Get in touch and share the basics — number of properties, locations, rough current values, and whether they are tenanted. We keep this completely confidential.

Step 2: We assess and make an offer. We will review the portfolio and come back to you within 48 hours with a written cash offer for the whole package. No obligation to accept.

Step 3: Legal and completion. If you accept, we instruct solicitors on both sides. We work with experienced portfolio transaction solicitors who can handle multi-property sales efficiently. You choose the completion date.

What about the tenants?

We buy tenanted portfolios and we are experienced landlords ourselves. Existing tenants can stay in situ — we honour all existing tenancy agreements and take on all landlord responsibilities from the completion date. You do not need to serve notice, manage moves, or deal with any tenant-related complications before the sale. Find out more about selling with tenants in place.

Frequently asked questions: portfolio sales

How many properties do you need to buy a portfolio?

There is no minimum. We have purchased single tenanted properties and multi-property portfolios. If you have two or more properties you want to sell together, get in touch and we can discuss what works.

Will you buy properties in different areas?

Yes. We buy across South Yorkshire, Nottinghamshire, Derbyshire, and surrounding areas. A geographically spread portfolio is not a problem.

Do all properties need to be in the same condition?

No. We buy portfolios with a mix of conditions — some properties may be well-maintained, others may need work. We factor this into the overall portfolio offer rather than requiring individual properties to meet a standard.

What if some properties have mortgage arrears?

We can work with properties that have mortgage arrears and can move quickly to prevent further deterioration of the situation. Contact us to discuss your specific circumstances in confidence.

How is the portfolio price calculated?

We assess the portfolio based on current market values, condition, tenancy status, and our costs of acquisition. We will always be transparent about how we have arrived at the figure we offer.
