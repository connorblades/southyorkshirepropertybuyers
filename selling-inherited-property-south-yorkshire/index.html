Selling an inherited property in South Yorkshire: a complete guide

Probate, empty properties, and family arrangements can make selling an inherited home feel overwhelming. Here is a clear picture of the process and your options at every stage.

Get a Free Cash Offer

What is probate and why does it affect the sale?

Probate is the legal process of administering a deceased person's estate. Before an inherited property can be sold, the executor of the will must obtain a Grant of Probate from the Probate Registry. This document gives them legal authority to deal with the estate's assets, including property.

Until probate is granted, you technically cannot exchange contracts on a property sale. However, you can market the property and agree a sale in principle while waiting for the grant to come through.

How long does probate take?

In straightforward cases, a Grant of Probate can be obtained in 8 to 12 weeks. More complex estates, disputes between beneficiaries, or incomplete paperwork can push this to 6 months or longer. The property may sit empty throughout, meaning ongoing costs for insurance, council tax, and maintenance.

What condition can I sell in?

Inherited properties are often sold in the condition they were left in. As cash buyers, we buy properties in any condition, meaning you do not need to make repairs, clear furniture, or spend money getting the property ready for viewings. If the property needs significant repairs, that is not a barrier to selling to us.

Why sell to a cash buyer?

A traditional sale through an estate agent can add months to an already lengthy process. As cash buyers we have no chain, no mortgage, and no survey requirements. Once probate is granted, we can complete very quickly. If multiple beneficiaries are involved, a clean, fast sale with a fixed offer removes many of the common friction points that cause disputes to drag on.
